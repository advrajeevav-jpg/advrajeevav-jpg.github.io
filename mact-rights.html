Your Legal Rights in MACT Cases

If you or a loved one has been injured or killed in a road accident, you are entitled to claim compensation through the Motor Accident Claims Tribunal (MACT), as per the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988.

1. Who Can File a MACT Claim?

  • Victims of road accidents (injured parties).
  • Legal heirs of deceased victims.
  • Vehicle owners, in cases of property damage.

2. Important Legal Provisions:

  • Section 166: Allows the victim or legal heir to claim compensation by proving fault/negligence of the opposite party.

3. Time Limit to File a Claim:

As per the Motor Vehicles (Amendment) Act, 2019, a MACT claim must be filed within 6 months from the date of the accident.

4. What Can Be Claimed?

  • Medical expenses (past and future).
  • Loss of income and earning capacity.
  • Pain, suffering, mental trauma, and loss of amenities.
  • Compensation for permanent disability or disfigurement.
  • Loss of consortium and funeral expenses (in case of death).

5. Documents Required:

  • FIR and accident report (from the police).
  • Hospital records and medical bills.
  • Death certificate and post-mortem report (if applicable).
  • Proof of income (salary slip, certificate, etc.).

6. Where to File?

The claim should be filed before the MACT in the area:

  • Where the accident occurred, or
  • Where the claimant resides, or
  • Where the defendant (vehicle owner/insurer) resides or has business.
